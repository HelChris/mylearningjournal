LEARNING STRATEGIES FOR LIMITED TIME

1. Use Active Recall: Start each session by writing down what you remember from your last session. This reinforces learning and identifies gaps in understanding

2. Code in Small Chunks: Break your projects into very small pieces that can be completed in 30-45 minute segments. This makes it easier to stop and start when time is limited.

3. Comment Your Code Thoroughly: Leave detailed comments explaining your thought process. This helps when you return to the code after a break.

4. Keep a Running Questions List: When you hit something confusing, note it down and continue working. Use your weekend sessions to explore these questions deeply.

5. Use Your Teaching Background: Apply your experience in breaking down concepts for toddlers to your own learning. Create simple analogies and explanations for complex programming concepts.
